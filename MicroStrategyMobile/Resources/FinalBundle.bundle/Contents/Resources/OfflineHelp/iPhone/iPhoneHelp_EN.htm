The following information is a high-level look at MicroStrategy Mobile for iPhone’s basic features.

Home screen

When you open MicroStrategy Mobile on your iPhone, the home screen displays, where you can browse the projects your iPhone is connected to. You can also navigate to different parts of the Mobile application using the buttons in the navigation menu on the left.

The buttons displayed in the navigation menu may be different depending on how your administrator has MicroStrategy Mobile configured.

The default navigation menu consists of the following options:

  • Reports: Displays the projects your mobile device is currently connected to. Tap a project to view its contents. Tap a report or document to run it.
  • Recently viewed: Displays a list of the reports and documents you have recently accessed.
  • Subscriptions: Displays all the MicroStrategy reports and documents you have subscriptions to. For steps to subscribe to a report or document, see the MicroStrategy Help.
  • Transaction Queue: Displays a list of documents that are pending changes that will be sent to your data warehouse once you are connected to the Internet.
  • Report Queue: Displays a list of reports and documents you chose to View Later.
  • Settings: Opens the Settings screen, where you can connect to new servers.
  • Learn more: Displays MicroStrategy's Mobile website.

Running reports and documents

When navigating a project, you can identify grid reports, graph reports, and documents by their icons.

To run a report or document, browse to it from the home screen and tap it. The report or document opens.

Answering prompted questions

Some reports or documents ask you to answer questions before the data can be displayed. The steps required to answer prompts depend on the information requested and the type of prompt.

Analyzing data

Once a report or document has run, there are several ways to interact with it to make analysis easier.

You can tap it to toggle between full screen and non-full screen mode.

In non-full screen mode, a header shows the back button, report/document name, and the currently displayed group of data.

Switch between metrics in a grid by tapping their titles.

Business data may be grouped (in a page-by) across the top of a report or document. To view another group, tap the currently displayed group of data and choose another group, or swipe horizontally at the edge of the screen to move between groups.

To zoom in or out of a report or document, perform a pinch.

Some reports or documents can be viewed in either a landscape or portrait orientation. Depending upon how the report or document was designed, you can change this orientation by rotating your device.

Some reports or documents allow you to view information at multiple levels. For example, a report may display time at the year level by default, but may be able to display data grouped by quarter or month. Tap an object to view its information on a different level.
