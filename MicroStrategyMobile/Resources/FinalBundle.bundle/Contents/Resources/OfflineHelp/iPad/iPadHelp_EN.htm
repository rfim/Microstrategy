The following information is a high-level look at MicroStrategy Mobile for iPad’s basic features.

Home screen

When you open MicroStrategy Mobile on your iPad, the home screen displays, where you can browse the projects your iPad is connected to. You can also navigate to different parts of the Mobile application using the buttons in the navigation menu on the left.

The buttons displayed in the navigation menu may be different depending on how your administrator has MicroStrategy Mobile configured.

The default navigation menu consists of the following tabs and buttons:

  • Home: Displays all the projects you have access to.
  • Recently viewed: Displays a list of the reports and documents you have recently accessed.
  • Subscriptions: Displays all the MicroStrategy reports and documents you have subscriptions to.
  • Transaction Queue: Displays a list of documents that are pending changes that will be sent to your data warehouse once you are connected to the Internet.
  • Report Queue: Displays a list of reports and documents you chose to View Later.
  • Settings: Opens the Settings screen, where you can connect to new servers.
  • Learn more: Displays MicroStrategy's Mobile website.

Running reports and documents

When viewing the reports or documents list, grid reports, graph reports, and documents are identified with a thumbnail preview. If the preview is missing, a generic icon for grid, graph or document is displayed.

To run a report or document, browse to it from the home screen and tap its icon. The report or document opens.

Answering prompted questions

Some reports or documents ask you to answer questions before the data can be displayed. The steps required to answer prompts depend on the information requested and the type of prompt.

Printing reports and documents

If your iPad runs iOS 4.2 or above, you can print reports and documents from MicroStrategy Mobile.

  1. While viewing a report or document, tap the Menu icon: .
  2. Tap Print. The Printer Options dialog box opens.
  3. To select a printer from your network, tap Printer.
  4. To select which pages to print, tap Range.
  5. To change the number of copies, tap the plus or minus buttons.
  6. When you are ready to print, tap Print.

Viewing and analyzing data

Once a report or document has run, there are several ways to interact with it to make analysis easier.

Business data may be grouped (in a page-by) across the top of a report/document. To view another group, tap the currently displayed group of data and choose another group, or swipe to the left or right to switch between groups.

To view a magnified view of data in a cell, tap and hold your finger over the cell. Drag your finger over other cells to magnify them.

Some reports or documents allow you to view information at multiple levels. For example, a report may display time at the year level by default, but may be able to display data grouped by quarter or month, or other dimensions, such as city. Tap and hold your finger over an attribute header or element and tap Drill Anywhere. To choose a dimension to drill to, scroll through the list until the dimension you want is in the center. Tap the dimension to view a list of attributes within it. Choose an attribute. When done, tap Update.
